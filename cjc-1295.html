CJC-1295 Peptide Guide

CJC-1295 is a growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH) analogue studied in peptide research for its ability to stimulate the body's natural production of growth hormone. It is often investigated for potential effects on body composition, recovery, and metabolic regulation.

What is CJC-1295?

CJC-1295 is a synthetic peptide designed to mimic the action of natural growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH). By binding to GHRH receptors in the pituitary gland, it promotes increased secretion of growth hormone (GH) and subsequently insulin-like growth factor-1 (IGF-1).

Two main forms of CJC-1295 are commonly discussed in research settings:

  • CJC-1295 with DAC – a long-acting version with an extended half-life.
  • CJC-1295 without DAC (MOD-GRF 1-29) – a shorter acting version often studied alongside other peptides.

Mechanism of Action

CJC-1295 functions by stimulating the pituitary gland to release endogenous growth hormone. Rather than supplying GH directly, it encourages the body to produce its own in a pulsatile manner.

This mechanism is why it is frequently studied in combination with growth hormone secretagogues such as Ipamorelin.

Areas of Research Interest

  • Growth hormone and IGF-1 stimulation
  • Recovery and tissue repair
  • Body composition and lean mass
  • Sleep quality
  • Fat metabolism
  • Exercise recovery

Research Dosing Protocols

In peptide research settings, CJC-1295 dosing protocols vary depending on the form being studied.

CJC-1295 without DAC (MOD-GRF 1-29)
  • Common research dose: 100–200 mcg
  • Frequency: 1–3 times daily
  • Often paired with Ipamorelin
CJC-1295 with DAC
  • Typical research dose: 1–2 mg
  • Frequency: once weekly

These dosing ranges are derived from published peptide research protocols and are presented strictly for educational and research reference.

Potential Benefits Being Studied

  • Improved sleep patterns
  • Support for recovery after exercise
  • Lean muscle maintenance
  • Fat metabolism
  • Improved overall metabolic function

Important Considerations

CJC-1295 remains a research peptide and is not approved for general medical use in many regions. Research into long-term effects, optimal dosing strategies, and clinical outcomes is ongoing.
